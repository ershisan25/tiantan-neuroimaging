Journey Group

Imaging biomarkers for neurological disease

We study imaging biomarkers for neurological disease, with a focus on multiple sclerosis and related neuroimmunological disorders, brain tumours, normative brain charts, and AI in neuroradiology. A central goal is to take these methods from research into routine clinical use. The group is based in the Department of Radiology at Beijing Tiantan Hospital, Capital Medical University (China National Clinical Research Center for Neurological Diseases), and is led by Prof. Yaou Liu (刘亚欧).

Research

Research highlights

Imaging biomarkers across neuroimmunology, neuro-oncology, normative brain charts and AI neuroradiology.

Imaging biomarkers in MS, NMOSD & MOGAD

Structural, functional and network biomarkers in brain and spinal cord for MS, NMOSD and MOGAD.

Novel neuroimaging biomarkers

Brain age, choroid plexus and blood-brain barrier (VEXI) as markers of disease.

Methodological innovation

Segmentation of white-matter lesions and fibre tracts, with detection of microstructural damage.

Molecular pathology prediction
